Reading your personal weekly horoscope is not as simple as glancing at your sun sign column in a magazine. True horoscope reading is a layered practice that incorporates your rising sign, current planetary transits, and the specific houses activated in your chart each week.

Step One: Know Your Rising Sign

Your rising sign — also called your Ascendant — is the zodiac sign that was on the eastern horizon at the exact moment of your birth. It represents your outer self, your physical appearance, and the lens through which you experience the world. For reading weekly horoscopes, your rising sign is actually more relevant than your sun sign, because it determines how the planets currently in the sky land in your personal chart houses.

If you don't know your rising sign, you'll need your birth time as well as your birth date and location. You can calculate it using any free natal chart tool online. Once you have it, start reading your horoscopes through that sign — you'll notice an immediate increase in accuracy and resonance.

"The stars incline, they do not compel. Your chart is a map of tendency, not fate."

Step Two: Understand the Weekly Planetary Movements

Each week, several planets are moving through different areas of the zodiac. Fast-moving planets like the Moon, Mercury, Venus, and Mars change signs frequently, creating distinct energetic themes that colour the week. Slower planets like Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto move through signs over months and years, creating long-running themes in your life.

The key planets to watch each week include:

  • The Moon — changes signs every 2.5 days, affecting emotional tone and daily energy
  • Mercury — governs communication, contracts, and travel
  • Venus — governs love, aesthetics, values, and money
  • Mars — governs action, desire, and physical energy
  • The Sun — moves through one sign per month, illuminating different life areas

Step Three: Match the Planets to Your Houses

This is where weekly horoscope reading becomes truly personal. Every sign corresponds to a house in your natal chart when reading from your rising sign. When the Sun is in Aries, for example, it's activating the house that Aries rules in your chart — and that house shows you exactly which life area is currently in focus for you.

Here's a quick reference for the twelve life areas:

  • House 1 – Self, identity, new beginnings
  • House 2 – Money, values, possessions
  • House 3 – Communication, siblings, local travel
  • House 4 – Home, family, roots
  • House 5 – Creativity, romance, children, joy
  • House 6 – Work, health, daily routine
  • House 7 – Partnerships, marriage, one-on-one relationships
  • House 8 – Transformation, shared resources, depth
  • House 9 – Higher learning, travel, philosophy
  • House 10 – Career, reputation, public life
  • House 11 – Community, friendships, goals
  • House 12 – Spirituality, the unconscious, endings

Step Four: Note Any Major Aspects This Week

Planetary aspects are the angles formed between planets in the sky. A conjunction (0°), trine (120°), and sextile (60°) tend to bring ease and opportunity. A square (90°) and opposition (180°) tend to bring tension, friction, and growth through challenge. When planets form aspects to each other in the sky, those energies interact — and when they touch a sensitive point in your personal chart, the effect becomes personal.

Putting It All Together

Reading your weekly horoscope well is a skill that deepens with practice. Start simply: know your rising sign, check which signs the major planets are in this week, and note which of your houses those signs rule. Then read any horoscopes from your rising sign perspective and feel how the themes apply to your current life.

Over time, you'll begin to feel the rhythm of planetary cycles and anticipate energetic shifts before they arrive. That's when astrology transforms from entertainment into true personal guidance.
